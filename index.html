Job Summary:

You are a Junior Project Manager responsible for the smooth functioning of day-to-day operations to ensure that projects are completed on time and within budget. The Project Manager leads projects and guides project staff. He or she implements the project plan and schedule, ensures safety and quality control, and delegates work to project team, contractors, and trades people.

Essential Responsibilities

• Manages and maintains project budgets
• Establishes contractual relationships for projects and ensures performance is consistent with those agreements
• Maintains effective communications with owner(s) and key project participants (project management, financial stakeholders, local government officials, and sub-contractors) of each project
• Ensures that projects remain focused on the letter and intent of approval documents and, when necessary, document changes
• Oversees the monitoring of projects costs and productions in order to ensure early recognition of negative trends such that corrective measures can be implemented ASAP.
• Reviews expected levels of documentation relating to standards for accounting issues, methodology of budget control, types of bids (fixed price, cost plus, unit price), etc. • Understands the tasks defined in the schedule and impacts on manning, overtime, utilization of equipment, and float time
• Ensures that the quality workmanship (including engineering and project management) meet or exceed standards identified in approval documents while remaining within the project budget
• Understands method of bidding, purchasing, negotiating, contracting, cash flow, expediting
• Understands and ensures that all documents used are complete, protect the owner’s interest, clearly define expectations and provide incentive to perform
• Keeps lines of communication open and effective among all project stakeholders
• Reports to LTS Leadership and owner on relevant information to completion of projects
• Ensures that safety and environmental standards pertaining to project activities are met or exceeded
• Sets expected levels of documentation, standards for accounting issues, methodology of budget control, types of bids (fixed price, cost plus, unit price), etc.
• Anticipates potential objections/issues in project; prepares short/long term solutions to issues
• Generates new solutions to overall projects problems
• Utilizing input from stakeholders, takes a lead role in researching, recommending, implementing, managing and updating and improvements to LTS Engineering tools, processes and procedures that support the business with meeting objectives
• May participates in meetings and strategy sessions with LTS Leadership team
• Leads and/or participates in focus groups, LTS Engineering initiatives and system upgrades
• Designs and documents specifications for Operations Manual or system changes; validates, tests and documents change in business procedures and system corrections, enhancements and upgrades
• Partners with LTS Engineering Training Group to align training matrix for employees to perform operational procedures and communicate changes in process requiring training support
